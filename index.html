    About this tool & how it works

    PlainSpeak scores text using the Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level and Flesch Reading Ease formulas — standard, decades-old readability formulas based on sentence length and syllable counts. Syllables are estimated with a heuristic (vowel-group counting), so scores are close approximations, not exact measurements.

    The jargon glossary is a manually curated list of common bureaucratic and legal terms, informed by general plain-language writing guidance (in the spirit of resources like plainlanguage.gov). It is illustrative, not exhaustive or officially endorsed by any agency.

    The "plain-language draft" is produced by mechanically swapping glossary terms and splitting some long sentences. It is a starting point for a human editor, not a finished or certified plain-language document.

    For context: WCAG (the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) includes a Reading Level success criterion recommending that public-facing text not require reading ability beyond a lower-secondary-education level unless an easier-to-read version is also offered. This tool is meant to help writers see how far their draft is from that target — it does not determine legal or accessibility compliance.

    • No text you paste is sent anywhere — all scoring happens in your browser's JavaScript.
    • There is no account, no analytics, and no server. This is a single static HTML file.
